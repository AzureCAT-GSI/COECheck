COE Assessment Report for {{assessment.partner}}

A Center of Excellence (COE) is a team of technical subject matter experts that help in the incubation of new technologies. You will likely have many different COE teams with varying specialty areas such as IOT, Digital Media, and Data Insights that reflect your business strategy as well as align to go to market initiatives. The COE group is not R&D, but rather builds upon innovation to land technology to drive business opportunities. This group provides technical insight from experience working with the technology and shares that knowledge with other roles and individuals across the organization. The COE should focus on the most strategic customer opportunities and/or strategic company initiatives, to ensure quality deliveries and to provide actionable best practices and automation that can be used with subsequent deliveries. The COE is not constrained to technical presales, but rather uses the technical presales experience to build the strategy and deliverables required to enhance an Azure practice capability. The COE is the pivot point between R&D and mainstream solution sales.

The COE maturity assessment model is a way to baseline and measure the progress and provide you with roadmap for improvement. The COE assessment report is a tool that is part of the solution development framework (SDF). The SDF is a methodology for identifying, designing and managing offers that can be used as part of larger go to market solution.

Key Assessment Areas

The assessment approach focuses on key areas that are common to most high-performing Centers of Excellence. The key areas are:

  • Readiness - activities support ongoing technical readiness across the organization. Improvement in this category helps to increase delivery capabilities
  • Delivery - activities enhance delivery capability through reusable IP and processes. Improvement in this category helps to decrease the cost of sale
  • Business Development - strengthen business development capabilities. Improvement in this category helps to increase business revenue

These categories help to discover your COEs best qualities and opportunities for improvement.

Scoring Criteria

The COE rating is an overall average of the scores within each section. Each section is measured on a 1-4 scale as follows:

Rating Score Description
Basic 1 No defined organization, relies on individual talent
Standardized 2 Automation and best practices with a loosely-defined organization
Advanced 3 Automation and best practices with a well-defined organization
Dynamic 4 Automation and best practices with a well-defined organization driving continuous improvement

Readiness

Readiness is a crucial part of the adoption cycle. Understanding and improving how your organization provides readiness to the field will pay huge dividends by increasing quality of solutions and decreasing cost of sale. By readiness we don’t just mean training, but refer to the entire process of awareness to mastery.

A key part of building a readiness plan requires establishing a core team of technical subject matter experts as a Center of Excellence. This COE group is an integral part of the incubation process and helping to land technology offers to the field. This COE group provides focus and a center of gravity to your incubation efforts.

Another key part of building an Azure practice is proactively driving readiness resources and improvement opportunities to the field. This helps build delivery capabilities while rolling out technology in a controlled fashion.

Once readiness is achieved and there are opportunities in the pipeline, your practice should incorporate Azure certifications. Microsoft certifications are validation of a practice’s capability and increase the likelihood that customer engagements will result in highly satisfied customers.

As your organization learns more about the platform and builds capabilities, you will undoubtedly recognize opportunities to improve the platform. Microsoft highly values this feedback, especially from our top GSI partners, and that feedback shapes the services and features that are released and helps Microsoft prioritize investment areas. Your feedback has a direct benefit to the services that you are incorporating into your solutions. Building a readiness plan that incorporates feedback builds a continually improving organization that is well-informed.

Business Development

The goal of the entire program is to help you grow revenue by building on the Microsoft Azure platform. Building a COE team will help increase velocity for sales opportunities.

Your COE team is not an elite sales team, but rather an elite team of technical subject matter experts that participate in the sales process to build field capabilities. The COE mentors field resources to build field skill and scale delivery capabilities. The COE team harvests best practices from joint opportunity pursuits back into the COE practice for dissemination at scale. As wins occur, the COE cultivates those wins by broadcasting them and incorporating them into regular communications to the field. This constant reiteration of past success is what drives the sales organization to have the confidence to propose new customer engagements. While volume of sales is certainly an indicator of success, it helps to provide key lighthouse wins that serve as a shining example of your practice’s capability to deliver. The E2E program will help you through the harvesting process and ensure your COE team is nurturing success by making the success easy to discover and to learn from.
